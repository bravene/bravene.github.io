How to maintain sexual desire in a long-term relationship

Sex plays a key role in our relationship. But the passion eventually passes, and many couples in long-term relationships eventually begin to experience difficulties in their relationships. sexual relations, namely in the loss of sexual desire. There is no need to get away from this problem, otherwise everything can end in disaster.

Here are five important topics that help couples maintain sexual passion.

1. Expectations

Our interest in sex for long periods of time the relationship naturally fades as we age and face various changes in life - the birth of children, stress at work, money worries or the death of a loved one. Researchers have found that people who accept these life changes as normal and natural, are more likely to experience them sexually satisfied. They don't view such changes as problematic, which seems to help them experience them smoothly. Others, who take everything personally, are more concerned, which leads to a negative impact on their sexual satisfaction.

This can also include the following a twist when one person has more sexual desire than their partner. And when couples recognize this difference, they are better able to adapt and accept this fact without negatively affecting their sexual desires.

2. Autonomy

You don't need to exceed the necessary proximity limits to avoid lose yourself and not completely lose yourself in the relationship.

A number of studies confirm the importance of having your own space in a relationship to increase sexual desire and passion.

Autonomy also gives us space to feel our thoughts and feelings separate from our partner, allowing us to cool down our own complex emotions on our own, and help our partner if such help is needed. It has been found that this dynamic increases relationship satisfaction, and consequently sexual desire.

3. The responsibility of the partner

When we try to please to our partner, knowing their needs, or making our partner happy, sexual satisfaction and sexual desire increase significantly.

Our desire and satisfaction will not increase if we have sex just to avoid negative consequences - resentment or resentment. swearings.

4. Novelty

Novelty in a relationship is an opportunity for sexual desire to grow. When it comes to sex, it can mean anything: new sexual positions, having sex in different places (or at different times of the day), or the use of something unusual and new in sex.

In some studies, couples who reported increased levels of sexual desire gave up their usual sex and varied it in various ways. Even making small changes to the decor of your bedroom will also favorably affect sexual health a wish.

The idea is to review your sexual interests to avoid sexual monotony.

5. Equality

Studies have shown that when couples are equally influenced by the relationship, sexual desire increases.

This equality does not mean that everyone is obliged to perform and bear responsibility for their half.

Equality in a relationship means that a couple should do household chores together or take equal responsibility for household chores.

The main thing is for both people to feel that they are making an equal effort in their work. the support relationship.

Of course, it would be pointless to try to apply to all couples those reasons that would affect the growth of sexual desire in their relationship. For each pair, this can be completely different reasons. In this case, you can only give advice. Talk more to each other about these topics, find compromises and be honest with each other.
